# 📊 ENIAC Discount Strategy Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes the impact of discounting on business performance at **ENIAC**. The goal is to evaluate whether discounts truly drive growth or negatively affect revenue and profitability.

The analysis addresses a key business debate:

* **Marketing perspective** → Discounts attract customers, increase satisfaction, and support long-term growth
* **Investor perspective** → Increasing orders but decreasing revenue raises concerns about profitability and brand positioning

This project uses data analysis to provide **objective, data-driven insights** to support decision-making.

---

## 📊 Big Picture

* **Timeframe:** January 1, 2017 – March 14, 2018 (~14.5 months)
* **Total Revenue:** €15 million
* **Total Orders:** 45,000
* **Unique Products Sold:** 6,000

### 🧹 Data Quality

* Raw data quality: **88%**
* Cleaned data quality: **99%**
* Data coverage: **25% of total transactions**

---

## 🧠 Key Insights

### 💸 Discounts Usage

* **92.4%** of products were sold with a discount
* Only **7.6%** were sold without discounts
* Most discounts are **small to moderate**, with few extreme cases

---

### 📉 Discounts vs Revenue

* Discounts are **widely used**, but:

  * No strong relationship between discounts and **revenue growth**
  * No strong relationship between discounts and **sales volume**

👉 More discounts do **not automatically mean more revenue**

---

### 📦 Product Segments

* **Budget products (< €250)**
  → High sales volume, but rely heavily on discounts

* **Mid-range products (< €1000)**
  → Strong revenue, but require higher discounting

* **Premium products (up to €10,000)**
  → High revenue per order with **minimal discounts**

* **Low-cost products (< €50)**
  → Least contribution to overall performance

---

### 🗓️ Timing Effects

* Sales peaks occur during major events (e.g. **Black Friday 2017**)
* Discounts during normal periods do **not significantly increase revenue**
* Timing is more important than discount size
